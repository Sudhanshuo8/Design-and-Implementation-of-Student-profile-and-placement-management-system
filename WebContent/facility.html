Facilities & Services
  

Dr.YVS Murthy Auditorium

An ultra-modern air-conditioned auditorium with a seating capacity of 300, almost totally built by the Dr. Y.V.S. Murthy Charitable Trust, Hyderabad. The trust had spent more than Rs. 2 crore on the auditorium, named after one of its alumni the late Y.V.S. Murthy, founder of the Alkine Metals Limited, who had done his B.Sc. (honours) and M.Tech. (chemical technology) from AUCE in the late 1940s. Andhra University Rector P.V.G.D. Prasada Reddy, in whose presence AUCE Principal G.S.N. Raju received the documents and keys of the auditorium from the managing trustee and son of the late Dr. Murthy, Y.S.R. Venkata Rao, said the auditorium was a wonderful contribution made by an alumnus and assured that the AU administration would keep the auditorium in a good shape and use it only for academic purposes. Dr. Murthy had promised to contribute Rs. 50 lakh for the auditorium when the then Vice-Chancellor B. Satyanarayana and others proposed that alumni of the college should do something concrete for the college and Department of Chemical Engineering, which was celebrating its platinum jubilee. Later, he doubled the donation and after his demise, his son Mr. Venkata Rao decided to spend Rs. 2 crore on the auditorium. There were contributions from other alumni like chairman of Green Park Hotel Harishchandra Reddy who donated Rs. 10 lakh. Dr. Murthy's wife Y.V. Lalitha Devi, and a number of his former colleagues and friends such as former Vice-Chancellors K. Ramakrishna Rao and G.J.V.J. Raju, Chairman of Visakhapatnam Port Trust Ajeya Kallam, Prof. Dakshina Murthy, Prof. Ch. S. Prasad, Dr. Subba Reddy, architect B.S. Murthy, Vijay Kumar of Vijay Nirman company, Vice-Principal of AUCE Sreerama Murthy, andRegistrar in-charge K. Samrajyalakshmi were among those attended.

ALUMNI OF THE COLLEGE

The alumni of the college have donated liberally for construction of an A/c auditorium with 300  capacity , an excellent hospital for the Engineering college worth more than ` 50.0 lakhs, a seminar hall worth of ` 25.0 lakhs. The other alumni are also eager to donate for the comprehensive development of the college to meet the global standards.


ACADEMIC SYSTEM

The college has introduced credit based grading system for all the courses at UG and PG level. In order to meet the global standards in scheme and curriculum, flexibility in learning is created by introducing free electives in all branches. Adequate importance is given to develop soft and communication skills in students. Opportunity for the students is created to involve in Sports, NSS, NCC and cultural programs.

STUDENT FEEDBACK

The student feedback for each teacher is collected every semester. Based on the feedback, the staff member whose rating is low are advised to improve teaching methodology. Every student has a teacher counselor. They are advised to meet their respective counselors as often as possible to improve their overall personality.

STUDENT SEMINARS

The students are permitted to attend and present the papers in the seminars conducted by premier institutes. They are also encouraged to conduct National seminars which provide the development of leadership, communication skills and skills in organizing methods.

SCHOLARSHIPS

The eligible students have been provided merit social welfare/tribal welfare scholarships from the Govt. of Andhra Pradesh. The PG GATE qualified students are given UGC/AICTE fellowships.
